üéì Academic Vocabulary

Research & Analysis

Analyze verb
Definition: To examine methodically by separating into parts and studying them
Example: "The researcher will analyze the data to find patterns."
Synonyms: examine, study, investigate, assess
Evidence noun
Definition: Facts or information that prove something is true
Example: "The study provides strong evidence for this theory."
Synonyms: proof, data, facts, support
Significant adjective
Definition: Important or meaningful
Example: "There was a significant increase in test scores."
Synonyms: important, notable, substantial, meaningful

Academic Writing

Furthermore adverb
Definition: In addition; moreover
Example: "The study shows positive results. Furthermore, it suggests new applications."
Synonyms: moreover, additionally, besides, also
Consequently adverb
Definition: As a result; therefore
Example: "The weather was bad. Consequently, the event was cancelled."
Synonyms: therefore, thus, as a result, so

üè† Everyday Vocabulary

Family & Relationships

Sibling noun
Definition: A brother or sister
Example: "I have two siblings: an older brother and a younger sister."
Synonyms: brother, sister, family member
Reliable adjective
Definition: Dependable; can be trusted
Example: "My friend is very reliable - she always keeps her promises."
Synonyms: trustworthy, dependable, consistent

Work & Career

Deadline noun
Definition: The latest time or date by which something should be completed
Example: "The project deadline is next Friday."
Synonyms: due date, target date, cutoff
Efficient adjective
Definition: Working well without wasting time or energy
Example: "The new system is much more efficient than the old one."
Synonyms: productive, effective, streamlined

üí¨ Idioms & Expressions

Time-Related Idioms

In the nick of time idiom
Definition: Just in time; at the last possible moment
Example: "I arrived in the nick of time to catch my flight."
Time flies idiom
Definition: Time passes very quickly
Example: "I can't believe it's already December - time flies!"

Success & Achievement

Hit the nail on the head idiom
Definition: To be exactly right about something
Example: "You hit the nail on the head with that analysis."
Break the ice idiom
Definition: To start a conversation in a social situation
Example: "I told a joke to break the ice at the party."

Common Verb Collocations

Definition: To decide something
Example: "I need to make a decision about which university to attend."
Take responsibility collocation
Definition: To accept blame or duty for something
Example: "Good leaders take responsibility for their team's mistakes."

Adjective Collocations

Definition: Intense rainfall
Example: "The heavy rain caused flooding in the city."
Strong coffee collocation
Definition: Coffee with intense flavor
Example: "I prefer strong coffee in the morning."
